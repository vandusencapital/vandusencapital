Life Insurance with Living Benefits

Life insurance with living benefits can offer more than a death benefit alone. Certain policy features may provide access to benefits during qualifying critical, chronic, or terminal illness events, helping policyholders and families navigate difficult times with greater financial flexibility.

What Are Living Benefits?

Living benefits generally refer to policy features that may allow access to a portion of a policy’s death benefit while the insured is still living if certain qualifying conditions are met. These features are often associated with critical illness, chronic illness, or terminal illness provisions, depending on the policy and rider design.

Why People Explore Living Benefits

Financial Flexibility

Some policyholders value the ability to potentially access benefits during serious health events to help with expenses, care needs, or lost income.

Added Protection Value

Living benefits can expand how people think about life insurance by adding possible use while alive, not just for a future death benefit.

Family Support

During major health events, these features may help reduce financial strain on loved ones and create more options for planning.

Important Considerations

Living benefits vary by carrier, policy, rider, eligibility criteria, and claim requirements. Not all policies include the same provisions, and benefits are subject to policy terms and conditions. Reviewing details carefully is important so you understand how the coverage may work in real-life situations.
