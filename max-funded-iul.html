What Is a Max Funded IUL?

Max funded IUL refers to a policy design approach that seeks to place as much premium into an indexed universal life policy as efficiently as possible within policy and tax rules, while still keeping the policy in force as life insurance. The objective is often to build cash value potential more aggressively than a traditionally structured policy, while preserving flexibility and protection.

Why People Explore Max Funded IUL Strategies

Cash Value Potential

Many people consider max funded IUL strategies because of the opportunity to build policy cash value over time based on indexed interest crediting methods.

Protection

Life insurance remains the foundation of the policy, providing death benefit protection while supporting broader planning goals.

Flexibility

Properly structured policies may offer flexibility in premium timing, access options, and long-term planning, depending on policy performance and design.
